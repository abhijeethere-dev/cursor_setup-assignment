**Topic:** AI Systems Misattributing Brand Trademarks

**Key Insight:** A study found all major AI systems making serious brand attribution errors — directly relevant to why brands can't just rely on AI to represent them accurately.

**Findings by platform:**
- **Claude Sonnet 4.6** — credited brand trademarks to rival companies in the first sentence of responses
- **GPT-4o** — described brand products as generic categories, omitted brand name in 74.2% of original responses
- **Google AI Overview** — fabricated a promotional expiration date, stripped supporting lab methodology from claims, and inverted a coverage exclusion into a promise the brand never made

**Why this matters for AI SEO:** This is a critical finding for B2B SaaS companies investing in AI-powered SEO content — if AI systems actively misrepresent brands, content strategy must include explicit brand entity reinforcement and structured data to correct the record.

Study Finds AI System Crediting Brand Trademarks to Rival Companies:

- Claude Sonnet 4.6 output credited four brand trademarks to four rival companies, with the error landing in the first sentence under a heading labeled "What It Is."
- GPT-4o described brand products as generic categories and omitted the brand name on 74.2% of responses during original characterization.
- Google AI Overview produced three distinct distortion modes: fabricated a promotional expiration date, stripped supporting laboratory methodology off a comparative claim, and inverted a coverage exclusion into a promise the brand published nowhere.
