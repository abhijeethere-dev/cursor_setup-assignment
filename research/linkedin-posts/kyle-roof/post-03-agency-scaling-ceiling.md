**Raw post text:**

There's a point where agencies stop growing. For most, it's around 15-20 clients.

Why? Because that's when the cracks in your delivery systems start to show.
- Client work starts slipping
- Quality control gets harder
- You spend more time fixing problems than finding new opportunities

It's not a sales problem - it's an operations problem.

The fix isn't just hiring more people (that adds complexity). The fix is standardizing 80% of your process, so only 20% needs to be custom.

When your audits, content plans, and delivery follow a repeatable workflow, you can handle 50+ clients without losing quality. That's exactly how I scaled my agency beyond the 15-client ceiling.
